About Madeline

Education & Professional Experience

Education Experience

I earned my Master's in Counseling in Clinical Mental Health Counseling with Drama Therapy from Antioch University in Seattle, WA in Fall 2021. My master's project included developing a Harry Potter version of D&D called Muggles & Magic for therapy purposes which I have done with various youth populations. I earned my Bachelor of Science in Psychology and my Bachelor of Fine Arts in Theatre from the University of South Dakota in Vermillion, South Dakota in Spring 2018.

Professional Experience

My counseling background involves wraparound intensive services (WISE) counseling, child and family counseling for anyone in the family system, school-based services, group social skill building, and group Dungeons & Dragons therapy. My experience has equipped me to work with clients experiencing a wide range of issues including depression, suicidal ideation, autism, anxiety, and attention deficit hyperactivity disorder.

Services

Individual Counseling

I'm a mental health counselor who believes in getting to know you and your interests first, then the healing can begin. I provide counseling services to teens and adults via telehealth or in person from the Elliot Bay office in Seattle, WA. I believe that being fun and interest-oriented is going to help find where you are now, so we can build the future you want to achieve - people never fail, plans fail, so let's make a new plan. You are the expert of your own life; I'm only here to help remember strengths that may have been forgotten or build new supports if helpful.  I know finding someone to support you on your journey can be hard; I'm honored to support you along your journey.

Counseling Approach

Information about how I approach working with clients

I have been trained in a variety of theoretical approaches and integrates these approaches for best client fit. I enjoy working from a trauma-informed geek therapy lens to gain a holistic view, highlight strengths, and build additional support. I utilize resiliency-centered counseling, narrative therapy, drama therapy and geek therapy.
